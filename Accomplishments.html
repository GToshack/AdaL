Her contributions to Computer Science

She is considered the first computer programmer
First person to see a numerical computational device to have so much more potential. She first gained prevalence in the scientific community with her translation of a paper about Charles Babbageâ€™s Analytical Engine, where her notes and insight nearly tripled its length (She was 27).
Her notes exhaustively discussed how a computer would or could be programmed. She stipulated that computers could be used for more than numbers, an idea that was not furthered until more than a century later.
